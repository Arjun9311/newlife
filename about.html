About Newlife Foundation India

Reaching the unreached - one child at a time.

Who We Are

Registered under the Indian Trust Act, Newlife Foundation India is a not-for-profit social development organisation. Activities are focused on development and empowerment of women and children. The foundation comprises well-experienced people in development, service to mankind, livelihoods (farm and non-farm), education, child development, and health sectors.

How We Began

Newlife Foundation was started in September 2013 with just 5 children. A group of social workers with a vision for reaching the unreached and helping the helpless gave them a hand of love and hoped them toward a happy life. Today, 20 children live with us as their home, and 70 more are supported in the surrounding community.

The Children We Serve

Children who have lost both parents, or one parent, or have been abandoned - aged 4 to 12. Among our 20 residential children, girls and boys each make up 50%. All non-residential children are from poor or slum backgrounds. All children study at Brilliant Master School, Nagole, Hyderabad.

“To aid as many underprivileged persons as possible to get education, which for them would not otherwise be possible. The trust is also interested in opening homes for the mentally challenged and the aged.”
Our Projects

Nine programmes that hold daily life, learning, and community together.

  • Orphanage / Children's Home: Residential care, education, and social welfare for orphaned and semi-orphaned children.
  • Education Support: Uniforms, books, fees, tuition, exercise, and music classes that keep children learning consistently.
  • Food and Health Care: Nutritious home-cooked meals and prompt access to local medical care.
  • Discipline and Structured Development: A daily routine that builds confidence, responsibility, and character.
  • Computer Education: Basic computer literacy so children are not left behind in a digital world.
  • Women's Development: Self-Help Group support for livelihoods, financial literacy, and independence.
  • HIV/AIDS Awareness: Community education to prevent transmission, reduce stigma, and connect people to support services.
  • Environment Development: Cleaner, greener, and more sustainable neighbourhood initiatives.
  • Home for Aged Men and Women (Planned): A future home for elderly people without family support.
