HIV/AIDS Awareness - Our Community Programme

Introduction

India has been working hard to eradicate HIV/AIDS, which poses serious health challenges to a large population. Efforts are being made to reduce the number of HIV cases to zero. However, India still has approximately 2.5 million people aged 15 to 49 estimated to be living with HIV/AIDS - the third largest affected population in the world.

What Is HIV/AIDS?

AIDS stands for Acquired Immunodeficiency Syndrome. It is a life-threatening disease caused by the Human Immunodeficiency Virus (HIV). HIV makes it difficult for the body to fight infections by attacking the immune system. There is currently no cure for AIDS, and a positive HIV test does not automatically mean a diagnosis of AIDS; diagnosis is made by a physician using clinical criteria and blood tests, including CD4+ counts.

Facts

Key information used in awareness outreach.

HIV infects cells of the immune system.

HIV can be transmitted in several ways.

35.3 million people live with HIV worldwide.

HIV is the world's leading infectious killer.

ART prevents HIV from multiplying.

Close to 10 million HIV-positive people had access to ART in low and middle-income countries by the end of 2012.

An estimated 3.34 million children are living with HIV.

Mother-to-child transmission of HIV is almost entirely avoidable.

HIV is the strongest risk factor for developing active TB disease.

India Timeline

How the national response developed over time.

1986First HIV case detected in India among sex workers. Government screening centres were set up.
1992NACP-I launched to slow the spread of HIV and reduce morbidity and mortality, with an IDA Credit of $84 million.
1999NACP-II launched as a 100% centrally sponsored scheme across 32 States/UTs and 3 Municipal Corporations.
2000Annual new adult HIV infections were estimated at 2.74 lakh.
2007NACP-III launched with the objective to halt and reverse the HIV epidemic in India.
2011New infections reduced to 1.16 lakh per year, a 57% reduction. Adult prevalence dropped from 0.41% in 2001 to 0.27% in 2011. Estimated people living with HIV reduced from 24.1 lakh in 2000 to 20.9 lakh in 2011, and ART saved 1.5 lakh lives by 2011.

Awareness Is Prevention

HIV infection is entirely preventable through awareness. Ninety-nine percent of India's population is uninfected, and awareness protects them. This is why the National AIDS Control Programme places maximum emphasis on information, education, and communication on HIV/AIDS prevention. Changing knowledge, attitudes, and behaviour is a key prevention strategy, and it is the strategy the Trust follows in our community.

The Trust's Role

The Trust runs local HIV/AIDS awareness drives aligned with NACO strategy. The focus is on education that reduces stigma, corrects misconceptions about transmission, and connects community members to government services and ART centres. The work is practical, non-stigmatising, and grounded in compassionate outreach.
