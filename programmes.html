Our Programmes

The Trust takes a holistic approach to child welfare - shelter, education, health, nutrition, discipline, and technology - while also serving women and the wider community.

Orphanage / Children's Home

Residential home providing safe shelter and a genuine family atmosphere for orphaned and semi-orphaned children aged 4 to 12. The objective is to provide relief and solace to the poor and needy by imparting education and social welfare. Every child is enrolled in Brilliant Master School, Nagole, a school within walking distance. Uniforms, textbooks, notebooks, stationery, and school fees are fully covered by the Trust.

Education Support

All children above the age of five are registered and enrolled in school. The Trust provides uniforms, text and notebooks, stationery, and school fees. A special tuition teacher conducts evening classes every day. Volunteers lead physical exercise sessions every morning, while music classes are held every Saturday and Sunday, ensuring no child misses learning due to a lack of materials or fees.

All our children study at Brilliant Master School, Nagole, Hyderabad - enrolled with full uniforms, books, stationery, and fees paid.

Food and Health Care

Children are provided with a balanced and nutritious diet prepared at the Home every day. The Trust ensures the best possible food quality within its means. Any health care issues that arise are addressed promptly by a local doctor. The aim is steady nourishment and reliable attention to health.

Discipline and Structured Development

Discipline is considered essential in every child's life. Children follow a systematic timetable from wake-up to bedtime. Evening tuition, morning exercise, and weekend music classes are all part of the routine. This structured rhythm builds confidence, responsibility, and character alongside academic learning.

Computer Education

Today, awareness of technology is a necessity. The Trust teaches children basic computer literacy so they are not left behind in an increasingly digital world. This programme is practical, future-facing, and tied to children's confidence in school and life. The Trust is actively seeking computer equipment to support the programme.

We are actively seeking computer equipment to support our children's digital learning. If you can help with computers or equipment, please contact us directly. This website does not process monetary donations.

Women's Development (Self-Help Groups)

Empowerment programmes for women in the surrounding community are organised through Self-Help Groups focused on livelihoods, both farm and non-farm. The work also includes financial literacy and social welfare support. The foundation's experienced team helps women build sustainable, independent livelihoods. This strengthens families as well as the wider community around the children.

HIV/AIDS Awareness

India has approximately 2.5 million people aged 15 to 49 living with HIV/AIDS, the third largest affected population globally. India reduced annual new adult HIV infections by 57%, from 2.74 lakh in 2000 to 1.16 lakh in 2011, while adult HIV prevalence dropped from 0.41% in 2001 to 0.27% in 2011. Wider access to Anti-Retroviral Therapy brought a 29% reduction in estimated annual AIDS-related deaths between 2007 and 2011 and saved around 1.5 lakh lives by 2011.

AIDS is a life-threatening disease caused by HIV, which weakens the immune system and makes the body less able to fight certain infections. There is currently no cure, but infection is entirely preventable through awareness, and 99% of India's population remains uninfected. The Trust runs education and awareness drives in the local community to reduce stigma, prevent transmission, and connect affected individuals to government support services.

Global context also matters: 35.3 million people live with HIV worldwide, HIV is the world's leading infectious killer, ART prevents the virus from multiplying, and mother-to-child transmission is almost entirely avoidable. The Trust treats this work as compassionate, factual, and local.

Environment Development

Community-level environmental initiatives aim to create a cleaner, greener, and more sustainable neighbourhood for the children and families the Trust serves. The work is practical and local. It reflects the idea that healthy surroundings support healthy development. Environmental care is part of caring for community life as a whole.

Home for Aged Men and Women (Planned)

This future programme is intended to provide dignified shelter, care, and community for elderly individuals with no family support. It extends the Trust's concern beyond childhood into old age. The vision is to serve every stage of life with the same sense of belonging and respect. It remains a planned programme, but it is clearly part of the Trust's long-term mission.
